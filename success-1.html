Second Success Story

Assiduous Boy Having Intrinsically Nuanced Acumen for Virtues.

Abhinav is the author of “Mapping Horizons: Solving the College Conundrum” a book written for international students to get scholarships at highly coveted American universities. He was recently appointed as the Youth Ambassador for World Wide Fund India, Yale Young Global Scholars Program, and AFS Intercultural Programs India.

As an avid researcher, he has worked on several research projects and has recently won the Space Challenge of the New York Academy of Sciences that dealt with the colonization of the moon. His enthusiasm and relentless ambition and resilience have helped him win various awards and accolades: All-Rounder of the Year Award, National Student of the Year Award consecutively for two years, NTSE, KVPY, National Rank 1 in National Science Concours, PRMO, Harvard Crossroads Leadership fellow, to name a few. Abhinav’s international educational expertise includes attending the Applied Science & Engineering session of Yale Young Global Scholars-2018 at Yale University, and BP Global STEM Academy 2019 in Egypt.

Abhinav is constantly eager to learn and an aspiring engineer.

When Abhinav was asked about the challenges he faced he said: “English wasn't my first language, so when I saw that American Dream, the language barrier was a prominent issue. Our economic situation didn't allow my parents to be able to pay for my education, so I had to solely rely on scholarships. It was like walking on a dark road as I couldn't be sure whether I would get the scholarship, but even attempting to get the scholarship to study at a top university in the US required me to give it all in and just focus on that. So, I didn't have a backup option in India and that period was stressful. I wasn't even sure if I would attend university. This stress deteriorated my health in turn.”

Abhinav got admitted to Stanford University Class of 2024 with a full ride. Standardized Test Scores: - SAT Subject Test score: 2400/2400 - SAT(R) score: 1520/1600. AP (Advanced Placement) Scholar with Distinction.
